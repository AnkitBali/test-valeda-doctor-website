Best Dandruff Treatment

A head has about 100,000 to 150,000 hair and each hair grows from a sac like structures called follicle. In fact, what we see as a hair is the shaft of the hair and its part that lies deep beneath the skin is the root. Interestingly both the shaft and the root are nothing but a dead appendage of the skin; the only living part is the follicle below the skin and surrounding the root. The follicle is nourished by blood & nerve supply in a junction area called 'papilla'. Most of the hair problems are the repercussions at follicle level, by causative factors in any of our functional organs.

1. What is dandruff? Is dandruff treatable?

Dandruff usually is a symptom of the medical condition seborrheic dermatitis. Dandruff is also considered to be the scaling of the scalp, the various causes of the disease being:

  • Scaling due to fungus Ptyris capitis or Ptyris candida.
  • Alteration in the sebum composition & quantum.
  • Endocrine disorders and genetic make-up
  • Unbalanced diet and constipation as indirect causes.
  • Excess work and lack of awareness and scalp care

4. Can only Anti dandruff shampoos and external applications cure dandruff?
Anti dandruff shampoos and external applications usually contain cetrimide, selenium sulfide or zinc pyridine thione to name a few. These active ingredients address the fungal infection only and may also help in making the scalp clean. Ironically the internal reason behind increased scaling of scalp and behind low individual capacity to ward off infection on its own remains unanswered. Moreover the fungus develops resistance to a particular synthetic active ingredient. The result is that anti dandruff shampoos and external applications alone are not the final answer for a complete cure of dandruff.

5. Can you give me some home care tips for dandruff?
  • Expose scalp to sun rays.
  • Keep the combs, towels and pillowcases absolutely clean as there are chances of re-infection.
  • Consume more fresh foods and drink plenty of water.
  • Avoid vigorous scalp massage, excessive stretching and pulling, regular use of hair rollers, emotional tension and anxiety.
